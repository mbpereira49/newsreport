A first map of the open-source AI stack arrives

Current AI’s Gap Map v0.1 catalogs hundreds of tools, models, datasets and hardware projects — and hints at a much larger uncategorized long tail.

Simon Willison flags a useful piece of infrastructure for people trying to understand “open source AI” as something more concrete than a slogan. Current AI — described as a global partnership building a public option for AI, founded as a nonprofit at the Paris AI Action Summit in February 2025 and backed by $400 million in committed capital — has launched Gap Map v0.1. The map details 421 products: 266 software tools and libraries, 85 models, 50 datasets and 20 hardware projects, produced by 228 organizations. They are organized into 14 categories across three layers of the stack: model components, product/UX and infrastructure. The excerpt also notes a much larger universe: 24,400 uncategorized artifacts in the long tail.

Why it matters: Open-source AI is often debated at the level of ideology. A map like this makes the ecosystem inspectable: what exists, where the gaps are, and which parts of the stack are actually shared infrastructure rather than marketing language.

Sources: Simon Willison
