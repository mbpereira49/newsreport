Gun-violence prevention gets quietly dismantled

As the Trump administration expands gun-rights policy, funding has been cut and prevention reports have disappeared from federal websites.

The administration’s gun agenda is not only about what rights it expands; it is also about what public-health infrastructure it removes. According to the Times, hospitals and community groups have lost funding, while government reports on preventing firearm injuries have vanished from federal websites. That combination suggests a policy shift conducted both through budgets and through the public record itself.

Why it matters: If prevention research and community programs shrink, the debate over guns becomes less informed by injury data and less connected to the places treating the consequences.

Record goods imports—including pharmaceuticals and data-center equipment—pushed the May trade deficit to its highest level in over a year.

The U.S. trade deficit widened in May because imports of foreign goods hit a record. The mix is telling: pharmaceuticals and data-center equipment were among the goods cited, linking the deficit not just to consumer demand but to health supply chains and the infrastructure build-out behind the data economy. The result was the largest monthly trade gap in more than a year.

Why it matters: Trade numbers can look abstract, but this one is a snapshot of what the U.S. economy is prioritizing—and what it still depends on other countries to provide.

05

Tencent’s new open model arrives huge, permissive, and hard to run casually

Hy3 is billed as a 295B-parameter Apache 2.0 Mixture-of-Experts model with a 256K context window and heavyweight downloads.

Tencent’s Hy Team has introduced Hy3, a new Apache 2.0 licensed model, according to Simon Willison’s write-up. The headline specs are serious: 295 billion total parameters, 21 billion active parameters, 3.8 billion MTP layer parameters, and a 256K context length. Tencent says post-training was scaled after feedback from more than 50 products and claims the model outperforms similar-size competitors while rivaling larger open-source flagships. The practical catch is size: the full model is listed at 598GB on Hugging Face, with an FP8 quantized version at 300GB.

Why it matters: Open-weight AI keeps getting more capable, but “available” increasingly does not mean “easy to run.” Hy3 looks like another step toward powerful open models that still demand serious infrastructure.

Sources: Simon Willison

The U.S. men’s soccer loss hurts because expectations finally changed

The defeat to Belgium disappointed fans, but the World Cup also showed how far soccer has climbed in American culture.

The U.S. loss to Belgium landed as a gut punch because the country now expects more from its men’s national team. The Times frames the disappointment alongside a broader shift: this World Cup highlighted soccer’s decades-long climb into the American mainstream. In that sense, the pain is part of the progress. A niche sport does not produce national anguish; a sport that has arrived does.

Why it matters: For U.S. soccer, the question is no longer whether Americans care. It is what the sport does with a fan base large enough to be genuinely angry when the team falls short.

08

How berries escaped the season

Driscoll’s helped turn a local, seasonal pleasure into a year-round refrigerator staple—and a global marketing machine.

Berries used to announce a season. Now they mostly announce that someone went grocery shopping. The Times credits Driscoll’s, the California giant, with helping transform berries from a local treat into a worldwide refrigerator staple and marketing juggernaut. It is a small food story with a big supply-chain shadow: freshness, branding and global availability have made seasonality feel optional.

Why it matters: The everyday abundance of supermarket berries is not natural; it is engineered by agriculture, logistics and marketing. That makes the clamshell in the fridge a quiet artifact of globalization.
