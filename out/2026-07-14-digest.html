New York is set to become the first state with a statewide moratorium on large data centers. Gov. Kathy Hochul will sign an executive order pausing construction of the largest projects for one year while the state assesses their effects on the environment and energy use. It is a blunt intervention in a sector whose growth is increasingly tied to AI demand and power constraints.

Why it matters: Data centers are moving from invisible infrastructure to political flashpoint. New York’s pause could become a template for states trying to balance tech investment, grid capacity and climate commitments.
